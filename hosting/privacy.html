WeVid Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 23, 2026

WeVid ("the app") is a location-based video app operated by Scimsoft ("we"). This policy explains what data the app collects, why, and what your choices are.

Data we collect

  • Account information. When you sign in with Google we receive your name, email address, and profile photo. You also choose a public username inside the app.
  • Videos and messages. Videos you record and post to the public feed, and video messages you send to other users, are uploaded to and stored on our servers.
  • Location. When you post a video to the public feed, the app attaches your device's location to that post so it can be shown to people nearby. Location is only captured at the moment of posting; the app does not track your location in the background.
  • Push notification tokens. A device token is stored so we can notify you when you receive a message.
  • Crash and diagnostics data. Anonymous crash reports are collected via Firebase Crashlytics to help us fix bugs.

How data is used

All data listed above is used solely to provide app functionality: showing nearby videos, delivering messages, sending notifications, and keeping the app stable. We do not sell your data and we do not use it for advertising.

Where data is stored

Data is stored with Google Firebase (Firestore, Cloud Storage, and Firebase Authentication). Google acts as our data processor. See Firebase's privacy documentation for details.

Who can see your content

  • Videos posted to the public feed are visible to any signed-in user near the posting location, together with your username and display name.
  • Direct video messages are visible only to the participants of that conversation.

Data retention and deletion

Your data is kept for as long as your account exists. You can request deletion of your account and all associated data at any time — see Delete your account. Deletion requests are processed within 30 days.

Children

WeVid is not directed at children under 13 (or the applicable minimum age in your country), and we do not knowingly collect data from them.
